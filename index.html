Recommended itinerary.

Built around a gentle start and a sane return. Ferry schedules can shift, so book the exact 11-ish outbound and 4–5-ish return online tonight.

9:30–9:45

Grab supplies near the hotel

Water, sunscreen, snacks, fruit, picnic bits, trash bags, towels. Do not rely on island food or shops — Lazarus is minimal.

Errand
10:00

Leave Albert Village / Albert Court

Take a 13-seater minibus if you want everyone together, or split into 2 Grabs. Drive to Marina South Pier is usually about 20 minutes from your area.

Transport
10:25

Arrive Marina South Pier

Collect/scan ferry tickets, bathroom break, drinks. Operators advise arriving 25–30 minutes before departure, especially weekends.

Buffer
11:00

Ferry to Lazarus / St John’s

Target an 11am-ish sailing. Singapore Island Cruise commonly lists weekend departures around 9, 10, 11, 1, 3; Marina South Ferries has weekend/public-holiday runs too, including direct Lazarus options.

Book this
11:35–2:30

Lazarus Beach picnic, swim, nap, games

Claim shade near the beach edge, set up mats, swim if conditions are calm, play cards/frisbee/kite, and keep it blissfully low agenda.

Beach
2:30–3:30

Pick one niche add-on

Walk the causeway toward St John’s for quiet views, detour to Kusu if your ferry route supports it, or hunt for the quieter coves on Lazarus for photos.

Optional
4:15–5:00

Return ferry window

Ideal: get back to the mainland before evening fatigue. If using Singapore Island Cruise’s loop, return may be via Kusu; if using Marina South Ferries direct, pick the matching return slot.

Return
5:30–6:30

Dinner or golden-hour add-on

Easy choices: Satay by the Bay for casual group food, Marina Barrage for skyline/kite views, or drinks/food around Marina Bay if everyone still has energy.

After

Cool niche things to fold in.

Pick one or two — don’t overpack the day. The beach plan wins because it stays simple.

🧺

Pre-order or assemble a proper picnic

Grab Korean/Japanese snacks, fruit, chips, sparkling drinks, and a cooler bag. Lazarus has limited facilities, so mainland prep is the move.

🐢

Kusu Island micro-detour

If your ferry loop stops there, visit the tortoise sanctuary and temple steps. It’s quick, odd, memorable, and very Singapore-hidden-gem.

🪁

Marina Barrage on the way back

Low-effort sunset skyline views, kites, and breeze before dinner. Great if the group isn’t ready to go straight back to the hotel.
